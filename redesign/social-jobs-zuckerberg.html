Social Page: The Vision

What happens when Jobs' obsessive minimalism meets Zuckerberg's engagement genius ‚Äî applied to accountability, not addiction.

üçé Jobs Principles

Jobs Typography IS the Brand

The "UNITY" wordmark is the only logo. No icons, no symbols. The gold gradient letterforms carry the entire brand identity ‚Äî just like how "iPhone" needed nothing else.

Jobs Ruthless Reduction

Every element earns its pixel. The composer is one line, not a card with tabs. Context switching is pills, not a dropdown. The tab bar has 5 items ‚Äî the maximum before confusion.

Jobs Moments of Delight

The "Perfect Day" gold glow. The celebration card's oversized number. The progress ring filling. These aren't decorations ‚Äî they're emotional rewards that make achievement feel tangible.

Jobs Information Hierarchy

Your eye flows: Logo ‚Üí Presence ‚Üí Feed. Within each card: Who ‚Üí What they did ‚Üí How to engage. Nothing competes for attention. The hierarchy is absolute.

üë§ Zuckerberg Principles

Zuck Presence = Urgency

The presence row (Stories-inspired) shows who's active NOW. Green dots create FOMO. Gold rings mean new activity. This is the #1 engagement driver ‚Äî "your people are here."

Zuck Social Proof Everywhere

Stacked avatar faces on reactions. "Sarah, you and 8 others." Momentum banner showing "4 of 6 checked in." Every signal says: your circle is moving. Are you?

Zuck Zero-Friction Engagement

Comment preview shows the best comment (drives more). Inline comment input. One-tap reactions. The celebration card has dedicated CTA buttons. Every interaction is one touch away.

Zuck Growth Loops Built In

"People in your circles also follow" ‚Äî organic discovery. The suggestion cards with mutual counts lower the trust barrier. Every user who joins makes the network more valuable.

‚ö° The Fusion

Both Accountability, Not Addiction

Unlike Meta's feeds designed for infinite scroll, this feed has natural stopping points. The momentum banner gives you the day's pulse in one glance. You check in, see your circle's progress, engage ‚Äî and you're done. Purposeful, not parasitic.

Both Premium Dark Aesthetic

Jobs would insist on the dark canvas ‚Äî it makes the gold pop, makes photos vivid, feels luxurious. Zuckerberg would approve the contrast ratios that keep you scanning. The glassmorphic cards float above the void. It feels expensive.

Both The Card System

Three card types, each purpose-built: Progress Cards (daily completions with visual rings), Post Cards (text/media with social engagement), and Celebration Cards (milestone moments with premium treatment). Jobs designs the visual system; Zuckerberg designs the engagement mechanics.
